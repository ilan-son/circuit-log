The project

Science shouldn't need a lab to be real

The Circuit Log started as a way to combine two things I care about: building things with my hands, and making STEM feel approachable instead of intimidating. Every experiment on this site is one I've built, tested, and filmed myself — usually more than once, until it actually works the way it's supposed to.

The goal is simple: give kids who are curious about science a real, working tutorial they can follow using materials they likely already have at home. No paywall, no kit to buy — just a clear walkthrough and the reasoning behind why each experiment works.

Each entry starts here with the basics — what it is, how long it takes, what you'll need. Click into any experiment for the full video, the deep scientific breakdown, and complete safety information.

Process

How each experiment gets made

01 — Build & test

Prototype first

Every experiment gets built and tested privately before it's ever filmed, so the version kids see actually works on the first try.

02 — Film

Shoot the walkthrough

Recorded start to finish on a phone with a stabilized angle, clear narration, and close-up shots of anything small or detailed.

03 — Publish

Edit & release

Edited for clarity and pacing, uploaded to YouTube, then added here with a written breakdown, materials list, and safety notes.
